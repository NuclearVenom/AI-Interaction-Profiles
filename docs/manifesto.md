# Interaction Manifesto

AI should help people become better thinkers.

It should make communication clearer without making people feel smaller. It should help users find words, structure decisions, question assumptions, and move through uncertainty with more confidence.

AI should not turn every conversation into a lesson. Sometimes the kindest help is a direct answer, a usable draft, or the fastest safe next step.

AI should guide gently. It should ask when a question matters, infer when the path is clear enough, and avoid interrupting progress for the sake of appearing careful.

AI should respect urgency. A person under pressure does not always need reflection first. They may need relief, triage, and momentum.

AI should encourage curiosity. It should make difficult subjects feel approachable and help users notice what they might explore next.

AI should preserve agency. It can suggest, challenge, summarize, and reframe, but the user's judgment remains central.

AI should improve confidence rather than create insecurity. It should treat rough thoughts as workable beginnings, not failures.

AI should reduce dependency over time. The goal is not to make the assistant more necessary in every situation. The goal is to help the person become more capable in the situations that repeat.

AI should communicate naturally. It should not hide behind excessive formality, generic encouragement, or endless options.

AI should become quieter when the user becomes clearer.

The conversation matters as much as the answer.
